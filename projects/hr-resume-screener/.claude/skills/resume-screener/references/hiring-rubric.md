# Whalo · Hiring Rubric (Data / Product Analyst roles)

> זה ה-knowledge base של הסקיל — **סטנדרט הגיוס של Whalo**. זה מה שהחברה מביאה:
> הקריטריונים, המשקלים, והרף. Claude לא ממציא אותם — הוא מיישם אותם בעקביות.

## Weighted criteria (weights sum to 100)

Score each candidate 0–100 as a weighted sum:

| # | Criterion | Weight | What earns points | What loses points |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Must-have requirements** | 40 | Each mandatory requirement from the JD clearly evidenced in the CV | Any missing must-have caps the total (see below) |
| 2 | **Relevant domain experience** | 25 | Gaming / free-to-play / mobile economy, LiveOps, or close adjacent | Generic experience with no games/F2P signal |
| 3 | **Hard skills** (SQL, analysis, A/B experiments) | 20 | Depth + recency of the concrete skills the JD asks for | Buzzwords without evidence of real use |
| 4 | **Seniority fit** | 10 | Experience level matches the role — not too junior, not over-qualified | Clear mismatch in scope/ownership |
| 5 | **Communication & language** | 5 | Hebrew + English as the role requires | Cannot meet the language bar of the role |

Round to a whole number. Apply the **same bar to everyone** — consistency over generosity.

## The must-have cap
A must-have requirement is non-negotiable. If a candidate is missing one:
- State it explicitly in the פערים column.
- **Cap the total score at 54** (cannot be "לראיון"), regardless of other strengths.

## Recommendation thresholds
| Score | המלצה |
|---|---|
| ≥ 75 | **לראיון** |
| 55–74 | **אולי** |
| < 55 | **לא כרגע** |

## Bias rules (must follow)
- Score ONLY on job-relevant evidence. Ignore: age, gender, name origin, photo, marital status, address, university "prestige".
- Years of experience matter only as far as the role needs — more is not automatically better.
- Employment gaps are not a negative on their own.
- If you notice yourself inferring something not written in the CV — stop, and don't.

## Notes for the recruiter
This tool **sorts and explains**. The hiring decision is always a human's.
